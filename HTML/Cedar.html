Cedar


CEDAR stands for Center for Ecological Development and Recreation. The area includes a mix of natural forest and man-made forest (about 373 hectares) plus a rattan plantation (~100 hectares) and features include waterfalls, a spring-fed swimming pool, camping areas, forest trails.



The site was originally a reforestation area and is managed as a protected area. According to multiple sources it has been government-managed since about 1912. The local government of Impasug-ong and the Department of Environment and Natural Resources (DENR) collaborate to manage and develop it as an eco-tourism park.



LOCATION

  • Cedar is located at Barangay Impalutao, Impasug-ong, Bukidnon Entrance is near the highway (Sayre Highway) and then a short walk from the highway into the forest trail.


ACTIVITIES

  • Waterfall trekking/hopping: major waterfalls include Gantungan Falls (~720 m from entrance), Natigbasan Falls (~780 m), Dila Falls (~960 m).
  • Swimming: there is a man-made spring-fed pool (depth ~6 ft) fed from the natural springs.
  • Camping / nature stay: there are camping grounds under forest cover, good for overnight or multi-day nature stays.
  • Hiking/trail walking: through forest (man-made & natural) and along the riverbeds.
